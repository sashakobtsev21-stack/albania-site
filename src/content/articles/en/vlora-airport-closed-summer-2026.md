If you were counting on flying straight into the south coast this summer, change the plan: **Vlora International Airport (VIA) will not open for the 2026 season**, and the charter flights pencilled in for it have been cancelled. **Tirana International Airport (TIA) remains the only operating airport in Albania**, so a road transfer to the Riviera is still part of the journey.

## What happened

According to Albanian Daily News (11 April 2026), the airport's launch has been put off because it **has not yet obtained certification** to enter operation. The infrastructure is reported as built, but the regulatory steps required to fly passengers are not complete — and without them the airport simply cannot open.

The knock-on effect is the **cancellation of charter flights** that had been planned for Vlora, including services from Nordic markets. Travel-trade sources quoted in the report had targeted a summer launch, then a later date, but both proved unworkable for the 2026 season.

## What it means for your trip

In practice you'll **fly into Tirana (TIA)** and continue overland to Vlora and the southern beaches. The drive from Tirana down to the Vlora area is roughly **2.5–3 hours** depending on route and traffic (treat that as approximate and check live timings before you set off). If your accommodation quoted a Vlora-airport pickup, contact them now to rearrange a Tirana transfer instead.

This mainly affects travellers who'd booked charter packages assuming a Vlora landing. Independent travellers are less exposed, but the same logistics apply: budget the transfer time, and decide whether you want your own wheels.
